A reverse mortgage line of credit can be an effective financial tool in retirement planning. Do you have a client who is retired or approaching retirement? It may be beneficial to add the reverse mortgage line of credit to their plan. Check out the scenario below to see how this product helped clients just like yours by providing additional funds.

Richard & Edith 65 $600,000 $125,220 Age Home Value Existing Mortgage Balance Richard and Edith are looking at ways to enhance their retirement plan. Their financial advisor recommended that they consider a reverse mortgage line of credit. In reviewing their mortgage information, they discover they only have four more years to pay on their current mortgage. The balance owed is $125,220. They find out they qualify for a reverse mortgage line of credit for the amount of $251,400. The line of credit pays off their current mortgage leaving them with $126,180 available funds, after closing costs, on the day they open their line. They decide to continue making their monthly mortgage payments, which increases the amount of their available funds. In five years, after they pay the same amount they would've paid under their prior mortgage, the funds available on their reverse mortgage line of credit have grown to $368,708. This gives them money to access later in their retirement to use any time and any way they like. ￼
